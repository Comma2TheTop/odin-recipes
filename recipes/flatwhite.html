Flat White Recipe

Description

A flat white is an espresso-based coffee drink that originated in Australia and New Zealand. It’s made with a shot (or double shot) of espresso topped with steamed milk that has a velvety, microfoam texture. Unlike a latte, which has more milk and froth, the flat white has a stronger coffee flavor and a smooth, creamy mouthfeel, with just a thin layer of foam on top.

Ingredients

  • 1–2 shots of espresso
  • 4–6 ounces steamed whole milk
  • Thin layer of microfoam (from the steamed milk)

Steps

  1. Brew 1–2 shots of espresso and pour them into a cup.
  2. Steam the milk until it is hot and has a smooth, velvety microfoam texture (not too frothy).
  3. Gently swirl the milk to keep the texture even.
  4. Pour the steamed milk over the espresso, holding back most of the foam.
  5. Finish with a very thin layer of microfoam on top.
  6. Serve immediately and enjoy!
